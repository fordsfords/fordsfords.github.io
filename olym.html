Wright Stripped of Gold Medal

Steven Wright was stripped of his Olympic gold medal in the hammer throw when a gross violation of Olympic rules was discovered early Tuesday morning.

"We did all the blood tests, all the equipment checks. I can't believe we overlooked something so obvious" lamented Charlene Clausen, director of oversight oversight. "I'm very disappointed that he wasn't more forthcoming with his background."

Steven Wright turned 42 on Tuesday, and Olympic rules prohibit the participation of old people. Michael Garwood, chief admissions officer for Olympic track and field events, says that the age rule is intended for the health and safety of the participants. "You know those geezers. They could fall down and break a hip. Or have a heart attack. I wouldn't want that to happen on my watch."

Steve Ford, president of AARP, protested the rules, claiming that the Olympic officials were just pandering to public pressure. "It's age discrimination, pure and simple. Nobody wants to see our flabby bodies bouncing around the track, showing those young whippersnappers that we've still got what it takes. Why, in my day people showed some respect for the elderly! Did you know that the Eskimos worship us like gods? Gods, I say! Why, I'll arm wrestle you! Right now! Come on, let's see what you're made of!" (This reporter politely declined the offer.)

Steven Wright was unavailable for comment immediately following the stripping ceremony, preferring instead to hobble to his walker and slowly make his way to his Oldsmobile.
