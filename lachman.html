My Start at Lachman

In the spring of 1994, I was unhappy with my Siemens job of 11 years (which is more than forever for a software developer). That was the year that the Chicago area was treated to an annular eclipse of the sun. I had prepared for the occasion by constructing a pin-hole viewer about three feet long. Then, over lunch, I spent a happy hour outside, watching the day turn an eerie semi-dark as the magic ring appeared in my viewer.

There were probably 20 other employees out that day (out of almost 1000), most of them with welding goggles. I think mine was one of two or three home-made viewers; it was certainly the geekiest one.

A little later that day, I telephoned my wife, Karen, who was working for a company called Lachman Technology. She told me that the entire workforce of Lachman spent the eclipse having a company-sponsored pizza eating and eclipse viewing party. About half of the engineers had home-made viewers, the other half used the milar glasses that the company owner (Ron Lachman) got from the Chicago Planetarium.

It was at that moment that I knew that I was working for the wrong company. A couple of months later, I was working at Lachman.

Where Is Lachman Now?

Alas, Lachman (the company) is no more - it has been eaten by Computer Associates. However, Ron Lachman is still alive and kicking. See http://www.lachman.org and http://www.flashback.com/www/september.1995/flashback/1361.uninews.2.2.html. The last time I talked with him, he had his fingers in 4 or 5 different companies.

Use of List

I have, on a few occasions, received mail from ex-lachman people expressing concern about the various uses of the mailing list. I have set up a system of preferences for each member. Each message is identified according to a fixed set of categories, and members can opt out of one or more categories.

Please e-mail back to me if you wish to NOT receive one or more of the following categories of mail:

  • NOUPDATES - don't send me updates to the list. One can always use the web page to look somebody up, but keep in mind that it is not complete - some people have requested to be removed from the web-based list.
  • NOJOBS - don't send me notices of available job positions.
  • NOLOOKING - don't send me notices of somebody looking for a job.
  • NOINVITES - don't send me notices of get-togethers (picnics, parties, etc).
  • NOEVENTS - don't send me notices of births, deaths, marriages, and other significant events in our members' lives.
  • NOADMIN - don't send me messages having to do with the administration of the list. (This survey counts as an ADMIN message.)
  • NOMISC - don't send me stuff that doesn't fit into the above categories. (e.g. "Does anybody out there know the answer to this question?")
  • NONE - keep me on the list, but don't send me anything. Ever. I HATE e-mail, and I never want to receive another one as long as I live! In fact, I hate people who SEND e-mail!!! They're all a bunch of techno- dweebs who have no lives and need the...
