Loch Ness Monster Sighted in London

LONDON, England (AP) -- "Nessie's on vacation" is what a lot of Londoners are saying these days. For the past few weeks, reports have been trickling in of a large green creature causing minor chaos in the underground.

"I didn't get a good look at him, but he was big," reported one regular of the Circle line. "Really big. And green, with a bit of yellow and white. Took up half the car, he did. Me wife, she thinks he's all fun and excitement. Meself, I think it's all just a big nuisance."


Nessie in the Thames?

The tube workers see him as more than a nuisance, however. "Our first concern is the safety of our ridership," said Nigel Whiplash, spokesman for the London Underground public relations. "He seems to be friendly, but we have no idea what he eats. We could be losing riders right and left and not even know it. I think he should go back to wherever he came from."

But Nessie doesn't seem to be leaving anytime soon, and most commuters seem to be taking him in stride. Lois Nickerson, a resident of Barbican Estates, says he's more of a pussy cat than a monster. "I give him a smile and a nod, and he settles right down. I think he might be learning to talk even. He makes the most God-awful noises, but I could swear he said 'cheers' the other day."
